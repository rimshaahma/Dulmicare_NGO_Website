Who we are

Dulmicare Foundation: Helping Community for a better tomorrow

Founded in 2023, Dulmicare Foundation is a not-for-profit organization (NPO) registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP) under Section 42 of the Companies Act 2017.

OUR KEY AND SUPPLEMENTARY OBJECTIVES

Poverty Alleviation

Combating and eradicating poverty by empowering the needy through skill & job potential enhancement.

Health Care

Facilitation of medical treatment to underprivileged populace, and improvement in healthcare facilities.

Education & Skill Development

Optimum promotion of technical, vocational & scientific education including sponsoring schools and supporting deserving and shining students.

Community Services & Infrastructure

Including construction of Patwar Khana, massive dredging of Dulmial Bun (pond) and reinforcements of its sides, graveyard cleaning/paved pathways, shuttle service for the poor, contribution to Quran Center and Masajid.

Preventive Health

Prevention of water-borne diseases through provision of clean potable water and promotion of measures to increase subsoil water levels.

Care Centers

Establishing care centers for the disabled, senior citizens, orphans and other such needy individuals.

Environment Preservation

Creating awareness and helping the public for preserving the environment.

Ethics & Moral Values

Promoting tolerance, harmony, ethics and moral values in the society.

Social Welfare

Supporting various social welfare activities to enhance community living standards.

Helping Community for a better tomorrow:
The Dulmicare Foundation Story

Founded in 2023, Dulmicare Foundation is a not-for-profit organization (NPO) registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP) under Section 42 of the Companies Act 2017, with Incorporation number, 0232649, SECP License No. 1811 and FBR registration No. A514235. Through education & skills development, poverty alleviation, medical care, environment preservation, and promotion of tolerance, harmony & moral values in the society, we aim to help communities for a better tomorrow.

DULMICARE FOUNDATION name was chosen keeping in view the affiliation of most of the directors of the company with their native village Dulmial. The Village Dulmial is a historic village in District Chakwal and is known for its contribution in the Armed Forces of Pakistan and British era. In recognition of its services, the village has a unique distinction and honour of having been awarded with a large caliber Artillery Gun which proudly stands at the village entrance. The village Dulmial gets its name from the village founder Mr. Dulmi Khan who was a military commander of Sultan Shahāb-ud-Din Muhammad Ghori. Hence the company name is Dulmicare Foundation. While the Foundation endeavors to bring improvements in village Dulmial and adjoining areas, the territories to which the object of the company extends, have been declared to include whole of Pakistan.

    Meet our Board of Advisors

    Aslam Malik

    Mohammad Aslam Malik, born to Seith Mohammad Ismail Malik, is 78 years old. He received his early education in Dulmial, his hometown, and later at Sir Syed High School in Katas. At the age of 18, he relocated to England, where he pursued further studies at Elephant and Castle College in London. Balancing various jobs, he supported both himself and his family in Pakistan, which included his parents and five siblings, all while continuing his education. Progressing through the financial services sector, he began as a sales consultant, steadily advancing to roles such as branch manager and senior branch manager. His dedication led him to consistently rank first among managers and sales executives across the UK. In 1994, EFU Life Assurance in Pakistan recruited him as an executive based in Lahore. There, he successfully established five new branches from the ground up. In 2000, he returned to the UK due to personal and family commitments. Since retiring, he actively engages in social and charitable endeavors. He served as chairman of the British Pakistan Association in Leicestershire for over five years, guiding its activities with wisdom before transitioning the leadership to a younger successor. He continues to contribute as an advisor. Mr Aslam has been married to Sorriya for 50 years, and with her unwavering support, he has been able to achieve a great deal. They are blessed with four sons, four daughters-in-law whom they consider as their own daughters, and five grandchildren. Mr Aslam's life philosophy is rooted in the belief that "when you feel pain, you are alive; when you feel the pain of others, you are human." Seeking guidance from Allah and the Hadith, he remains committed to serving humanity until his last breath, Insha'Allah.

    Dr Asif

    Dr. Khan received his BSc (Hons) and MSc in Earth Sciences from the University of Peshawar (1975-1981), followed by his Ph.D. from Imperial College, London, UK in 1988.     General Asif

    Lt. Gen. Muhammad Asif, Hilal-e-Imtiaz (M), was born in 1958. He attended Government Middle School Dulmial and later graduated with distinction from Sir Syed High School, Katas. He completed his FSc from Government Degree College, Rawalpindi, and joined the Pakistan Military Academy at Kakul in 1976. During his military service, he earned a Master's degree in Strategic Studies from Quaid-e-Azam University, Islamabad. Commissioned in 1979 into the 44th Baluch Regiment (later converted to the 6th Sind Regiment), Lt. Gen. Asif had a distinguished military career spanning 39 years. He served in various command, staff, and instructional roles. He also participated in the UN mission in Cambodia and served as a Defense Adviser in Moscow for three years. After retiring from the Joint Staff Headquarters in 2016, he settled in Islamabad. In his leisure time, he enjoys playing golf, reading books, and traveling.

    Mr Khurshid

    Mr Khurshid was born in the village of Dulmial, District Chakwal, Pakistan. He received his primary school education in Dulmial and Kattas Schools. He went on to receive his Bachelor of Commerce from Karachi University. He pursued his professional designation as a Chartered Accountant (CA) from the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales. Khurshid completed his CA Training/Articleship in London and after qualification joined the prestigious firm of KPMG. At this juncture, he made a big decision of his life and relocated from London, UK to Toronto, Canada joining Deloitte in their Toronto office. After leaving the firm, he worked with the Federal Government of Canada in the Auditor General Office of Canada. The most significant move of Khurshid's career came in 1981 when he decided to shift to Saudi Arabia to join Lockheed Martin (an American Aerospace Company). In 1987, he was promoted to the position of Chief Financial Officer and in 1990, he took on the additional responsibilities of Deputy General Manager. During his career at Lockheed, he was selected to attend the Lockheed Management Institute in 1985 and the Lockheed Leadership Institute in 1989, both programs designed to develop future leaders within the organization. After 21 years with Lockheed Martin, Khurshid diversified his career path, joining a group headed by a Senior member of the Saudi Royal Family in the capacity of Senior Financial Advisor. His role was to advise on investment decisions, serve as a member of the Board of Directors, and took a leadership role in restructuring the Group operating companies. He also served as volunteer Chairman of the Pakistan Accountants Forum, Saudi Arabia, for ten years. Khurshid is married and has four children. He now resides in Toronto, Canada. He is an avid golfer. Currently, he looks after business interests of the family and manages the family investments.

    Achievements

    Poverty Alleviation

    Provision of social and financial support to a large number of families from the underprivileged segment of society. Includes monthly financial assistance to widows and deserving families, marriage assistance, financial support for jobless individuals for startups, distribution of winter clothing, and provision of substantial quantities of Ration Packs to deserving low-income families during Ramadan.

    Education and Skill Development

    For improving the quality of education & the infrastructure of Public Schools in Dulmial/elsewhere, Boys and Girls Primary and Secondary Schools have been given large amounts, support programmes were sponsored e.g financial assistance primarily from Dulmial and adjoining villages, at various Colleges and universities. DULMICARE established a vocational Training Institute (VTI) Dulmial with the support of MOAWIN FOUNDATION, which is conducting Dress Designing and Making courses for girls since Aug 2022. The VTI also successfully conducted a graphic Designing Course in 2022/2023 and has recently conducted an E Commerce Course at Choa saiden Shah with the support and collaboration of Mr. Dawood Gulzar. Efforts are also in hand to enhance the scope and scale of DULMICARE FOUNDATION VTI with the collaboration of The HUNAR FOUNDATION (THF).

    Health and Medical Facilities

    Facilitated major surgical procedures and other medical treatment of a number of needy patients at local hospitals across Pakistan, financial assistance to chronic patients for life saving medical support, Medical Camps. Additionally signed a written MOU with Trauma Center, THQ Hospital Kallar Kahar and sponsored expenses of its Dialysis center. In this regard donated millions of rupees for running expenses of the Dialysis center including required medicines, kits, consumables, maintenance of machines etc. recently has established Fateh Dulmicare Medical Centre at Village Dulmail with the support and patronage of Mr. Khurshid Ahmad of Dulmial; presently residing in Canada.

    Community Services & Infrastructure

    Apart from Education, Health and Poverty Alleviation sectors, the DULMICARE FOUNDATION has sponsored following activities:
    a. Construction of Patwar Khana / Dehi Markaz Mall building at Village Dulmial. The center is functioning successfully since June 2023.
    b. Massive dredging of Dulmial Bun (pond) and reinforcements of its sides along with land reclamation for widening of adjoining road was undertaken in 2022.
    c. Cleaning of weeds and wild growth from village Graveyard, an activity sponsored by Mr. Abdul Muqeet residing in UK. Work on paved pathways and other ancillary requirement is in progress. Efforts are also in hand to streamline burial arrangements.
    d. Contributions to various Masajid on regular basis.
